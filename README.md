# AgriHerders
Model of Agricultures and Herders in Benin
In this model, there is 2 types of human actors: the farmers who cultivate (Casava, Soja, Cotton, Maize), and the herders who manage herds of cows. 
When grazing in a field, cows consume biomass (mainly crop residues) and help to improve soil fertility through their manure.
If they are in good health, cows reproduce, leading to an increase in herd size, which in turn requires more space for grazing.
Conversely, population growth forces farmers to use more land for agriculture.
Although they have mutual interests, the actions of farmers and herders contribute to two processes that lead to land saturation and conflicts.
